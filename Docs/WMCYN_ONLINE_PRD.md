# WMCYN Online PRD v0.3
## The WMCYN Crib: First Location of WMCYN Online
**Owner:** Jared / WMCYN
**Project:** WMCYN Online
**First Playable Location:** The WMCYN Crib
**Current Phase:** Discovery + Spec-Driven Planning
**Target Milestone 1:** First Signal Build by August 7, 2026
**Target Milestone 2:** WMCYN Crib Private Alpha by end of September 2026
**Primary Build Targets:** PCVR + standalone VR headsets
**Development Philosophy:** Core systems in C++, rapid iteration where appropriate
**Primary MVP Customer:** WMCYN itself

# 1. Product Summary
WMCYN Online is a long-term online VR world that starts with one real, meaningful place: **The
WMCYN Crib**.
The Crib already exists as part of WMCYN's VRChat world. The next step is to port, rebuild, and
evolve it inside Unreal Engine as the first canonical location of WMCYN Online.
This first version is not trying to launch the full metaverse, full MMORPG, public social platform,
creator economy, concert venue, storefront system, or massive open world. Those are future
layers.
The MVP is one room/area: **The WMCYN Crib**.
The Crib is a digital home base where WMCYN members and collaborators enter as account-bound
avatars, hang out, record content, create conversations, create Verbatim moments, test identity
systems, and begin building the lore of WMCYN Online.
The audience may first discover the Crib through clips, interviews, behind-the-scenes content,
avatar moments, and social posts. At first, it may look like a different kind of VR podcast or
digital hangout. Over time, people should realize the Crib is not just a backdrop. It is the first
address inside a larger online world.
The product strategy is to build the world from culture outward.

## First Signal Canonical Target

Bring The WMCYN Crib online with three standalone Quest users and one PCVR recording user, all present in the same always-on world, with basic presence, voice, and OBS-friendly capture from the PCVR machine.

Terminology update:

* The Crib is the anchor. Do not frame it as a disposable private copy.
* Users are persistent identities.
* Presence is a user's live in-world embodiment.
* First Signal has three standalone Quest presences and one PCVR recording presence.
* Recording is a temporary capability on the PCVR user, not a permanent user class.
* First Signal login is username or email, password, and Enter World.
* First Signal enters the persistent Crib world through username-or-email/password login.
* Initial First Signal accounts are provisioned manually; public registration is out of scope.
* Avoid lead/visitor/operator role language for current scope unless describing older source material.

For MVP, the primary customer is **WMCYN itself**.
The product succeeds if the WMCYN team prefers creating, recording, and building lore inside
the Crib instead of relying only on disconnected real-world workflows, third-party platforms, or

team and close collaborators.

The MVP is successful when the Crib becomes WMCYN's preferred place to create
avatar-based content, create conversations, record moments, and build the lore of WMCYN
Online.

# 6. Product Strategy
The product should grow in layers.
## Layer 1: The Crib
One room. One recognizable place. One strong identity.
The Crib is where WMCYN records, hangs out, tests avatars, invites collaborators, and creates content.

## Layer 2: Identity
Users have accounts. Accounts have avatars. Avatars belong to people. Live users have presence modes and temporary capabilities.
This creates continuity.
## Layer 3: Content
In-world moments become clips, Verbatim posts, live streams, and mobile app feed items.
The world creates the content.
## Layer 4: Access
People first watch the world from the outside. Then they want to enter. Access becomes
meaningful because the Crib already has lore.
## Layer 5: Expansion
The Crib can eventually connect to stores, event rooms, watch rooms, creator spaces, and
public meetups.
## Layer 6: Ecosystem
The world, mobile app, backend, content pipeline, merch, avatar identity, and events all become
part of one WMCYN system.

# 7. Core Product Loop
The Crib exists to create moments that turn into content, curiosity, and eventual demand for
access.
## Creation Loop
1. The WMCYN Crib is brought online.
2. Users log in and enter the Crib as account-bound avatars.
3. Two users enter from standalone VR headsets.
4. One user enters from PCVR with recording capability.
5. Conversation or hangout happens naturally.
6. The PCVR machine records the world through OBS-friendly capture.
7. Strong moments are marked as Verbatim candidates.
8. Clips are created from the recording.

9. Clips are shared on YouTube, Instagram, X, and future WMCYN mobile app surfaces.
10. Audience becomes curious about the Crib.
11. Audience wants access to WMCYN Online.
## Core Loop Statement
The Crib creates content.
The content creates curiosity.
Curiosity creates demand for access.
Access grows the world.

# 10. MVP Presence Modes and Capabilities
## 10.1 PCVR Recording Presence
**Platform:** PCVR
**Primary Purpose:** Capture the scene from the PCVR machine
The recording user enters in VR and carries recording/capture capability for First Signal.
Capabilities:
* enter the Crib
* view all standalone VR users
* provide OBS-friendly capture from the PCVR machine
* switch between saved camera angles
* use iPhone/VCam workflow when available
* hide UI for OBS capture
* mark Verbatim moments
* see room/world runtime status
* possibly manage basic recording capabilities later
The PCVR recording user should not be treated as the permanent server authority.

## 10.2 Standalone VR Presence
**Platform:** standalone VR headset
**Primary Purpose:** Participate in the Crib as an embodied user

Capabilities:
* enter the Crib
* load account-bound avatar
* sit or stand in a marked position
* interact with microphones or props
* speak naturally
* be visible to the PCVR recording user
* participate in conversation or hangout flow

## 10.3 Temporary Capabilities
Capabilities are not permanent user types. They can be granted to a user presence for a world runtime.

Capabilities:
* `Recording`
* `CanTriggerVerbatimMarker`
* `CanManageCapture`
* future moderation/admin capabilities
## 10.4 Observer
**Platform:** PCVR or standalone headset later
**Primary Purpose:** Watch or attend without controlling the world runtime
Capabilities:
* enter the Crib
* watch from a defined area
* limited interaction
* no production controls
Observer can be later scope.
## 10.5 Admin
**Platform:** PC primarily
**Primary Purpose:** Manage access, capabilities, and world runtime state

Capabilities:
* assign capabilities
* manage world access later
* kick/mute users later
* assign seats
* start/end recording or world-runtime state later
* control permissions
Admin can overlap with the PCVR recording user in MVP.

# 11. Primary MVP User Flow
## Flow: Entering the WMCYN Crib
1. User opens WMCYN Online.
2. User lands on a simple login screen.
3. User enters username or email and password.
4. User selects Enter World.
5. System identifies the user.
6. System stores display name, presence mode, and temporary capabilities.
7. System loads the user's assigned avatar or First Signal placeholder.
8. User enters The WMCYN Crib in `L_WMCYNOnline`.
9. User spawns in the correct area based on presence/capability.
10. Username/display name appears above or near the avatar.
11. Two users enter from standalone VR headsets.
12. One user enters from PCVR with recording capability.
13. Users hang out, sit, talk, interact, or record.
14. The PCVR machine captures the world.
15. Strong moments are marked as Verbatim candidates.
16. Users leave when finished; the persistent Crib remains the world.
17. World event metadata is saved locally or prepared for backend later.
---
# 12. PCVR Recording Flow
1. Open WMCYN Online PCVR build.
2. Enter The WMCYN Crib.
3. Login with username-or-email/password and confirm recording capability.
4. See list of connected users.
5. Confirm all three standalone Quest users are present.
6. Switch to recording/capture mode if needed.
7. Choose saved shot:

* wide Crib shot
* couch shot
* standalone VR user close-up
* two-user shot
* recording user view or supported camera feed
8. Hide UI for OBS.
9. Start recording in OBS.
10. Optionally use iPhone/VCam for cinematic movement.
11. Mark strong lines or moments as Verbatim markers.
12. End recording.
13. Leave the world when finished.
14. Save recording notes.
---
# 13. Standalone VR User Flow
1. Open WMCYN Online standalone headset build.
2. Login with username or email and password.
3. Select Enter World.
4. Load assigned avatar.
5. Spawn inside the Crib.
6. Move to the couch, seat, or hangout area.
7. Interact with microphone, props, or environment.
8. Talk naturally with other users.
9. Appear correctly to the PCVR recording user.
10. Leave the world when finished.

# 14. Operational Workflow
The Crib must support an actual repeatable production workflow, not just a playable test.
## 14.1 Before Recording Run
Before a Crib recording run, the team should:
* confirm the persistent Crib world is reachable
* confirm username-or-email/password login works
* assign capabilities
* assign avatars
* confirm standalone VR users are ready

* confirm PCVR recording user is ready
* confirm voice workflow
* confirm OBS scene/capture
* confirm recording storage location
* confirm recording goal
* confirm any planned Verbatim themes or topics
## 14.2 During Recording Run
During a Crib recording run, the team should:
* users enter the Crib
* standalone VR users get into position
* PCVR recording user confirms the capture frame
* recording starts
* Verbatim moments are marked
* connection quality is monitored
* voice quality is monitored
* the conversation continues naturally
* producer avoids interrupting the flow unless needed
## 14.3 After Recording Run
After a Crib recording run, the team should:
* save recording
* create recording notes
* review Verbatim timestamps
* select clips
* edit clips
* post, archive, or schedule content
* log what worked
* log what broke
* update Apple Notes with decisions and learnings

# 17. Product Requirements
## 17.1 Account / Identity Requirements
The system must support a basic identity model.
MVP identity fields:
* UserId
* DisplayName
* PresenceMode
* Capabilities
* AvatarId
* Platform
* WorldRuntimeId
For MVP, identity can be dev-only or local/mock backend.
Acceptance criteria:
* each user has a visible display name
* each user has a presence mode
* the PCVR recording user can receive recording capability
* each user can be assigned an avatar
* the system can distinguish standalone VR users from the PCVR recording user
## 17.2 Avatar Requirements

Avatars must be tied to user identity.
MVP avatar rule:
* one avatar per user
* avatars are assigned manually
* no avatar editor
* no user uploads
* no public avatar marketplace
* no outfit customization
* no complex inventory
* no full creator avatar pipeline
The goal is not avatar freedom yet.
The goal is identity continuity.
MVP avatar requirements:
* each user loads an assigned avatar
* avatar appears to other connected users
* standalone VR and PCVR users show head and hand presence
* avatar can support seated or standing state
* avatar has a display name or nameplate toggle
* avatar system supports future platform variants
Acceptance criteria:
* Jared can enter and appear as Jared's assigned avatar
* standalone VR users can enter and appear as assigned avatars
* PCVR recording user can see standalone VR avatars in the room
## 17.3 Presence and Capability Requirements
The system must support presence/capability-based behavior.
MVP presence modes:
* Quest
* PCVR

MVP capabilities:
* Recording
* CanTriggerVerbatimMarker
* Observer
* Admin

Acceptance criteria:
* PCVR user can be assigned Recording capability
* standalone headset users can be assigned Quest presence
* presence/capability determines spawn behavior and UI
* presence/capability state can be stored in PlayerState or equivalent identity structure
## 17.4 Room Requirements
The MVP room is the WMCYN Crib.
The room should include:
* couch
* banner wall
* recognizable Crib layout
* spawn points
* PCVR recording position
* standalone VR user positions
* hangout space
* basic props
* simple lighting
* collision
* standalone headset performance considerations
Acceptance criteria:
* users spawn into the Crib
* couch and banner wall are visible
* the room feels connected to the existing VRChat Crib
* standalone headset build can run the room at an acceptable performance baseline
## 17.5 Networking Requirements
The system must support standalone headset + PCVR users in the same world.
MVP networking requirements:
* PCVR client can enter the world
* standalone headset clients can enter the world
* users can see each other
* avatar position replicates

* VR head/hands replicate enough for presence
* basic interaction state replicates
* world runtime can be tested locally first
* architecture should not depend forever on the PCVR machine being server authority
Acceptance criteria:
* PCVR user and at least one standalone headset user are in the same world
* PCVR user sees standalone headset avatar movement
* standalone headset users see other connected users
* at least one object or state change replicates
* disconnect does not permanently break world presence
## 17.6 Voice Requirements
Voice is a first-class MVP system because the Crib is built around conversation.
The team must choose one MVP voice path.
### Option A: External Voice / Real Mics
Users speak through real microphones, Discord, or local recording setup. Unreal provides
visuals and presence.
Pros:
* best audio quality
* easiest for content
* safest for early recording
Cons:
* less native world immersion
* requires separate audio workflow
### Option B: In-Game Voice
Users speak through standalone headset/PCVR voice chat inside Unreal.
Pros:
* more immersive
* closer to future public world behavior

Cons:
* harder to build
* harder to record cleanly
* more networking/audio complexity
### Option C: Hybrid
Use external/real mics for final recording and in-game voice for presence/reference.
Recommended MVP choice: **Option C Hybrid**.
For early content, clean audio matters more than perfect native voice infrastructure.
MVP voice requirements:
* VR users can hear each other
* PCVR recording user can monitor conversation
* final recording has usable audio
* mute workflow is understood
* voice method is documented before recording runs
Acceptance criteria:
* all participants understand which voice workflow is being used
* VR users can communicate clearly
* PCVR recording user can monitor the world audio
* recorded audio is usable for content
## 17.7 PCVR Recording Requirements
The PCVR build must support content capture.
MVP camera requirements:
* PCVR user can enter recording mode or receive recording capability
* PCVR user can switch to saved camera angles if supported
* PCVR user can hide UI
* PCVR output can be captured by OBS
* camera can frame couch/banner wall cleanly
Saved camera targets:
* wide Crib shot

* couch shot
* standalone VR user shot
* two-user shot
* supported recording/camera feed
Acceptance criteria:
* PCVR recording user can frame a usable shot
* OBS can capture footage
* UI can be hidden for clean output
* one 15-30 second test clip can be recorded
## 17.8 Verbatim Requirements
Verbatim should start as a marker system.
MVP Verbatim requirements:
* PCVR recording user or another permitted user can mark a moment
* marker includes timestamp/world runtime reference
* marker can be stored locally or prepared for backend
* marker represents a future clip-worthy moment
Acceptance criteria:
* user can press "Mark Verbatim Moment"
* system creates a structured event
* event contains WorldRuntimeId, UserId, PresenceMode, timestamp, and type
## 17.9 Analytics / World Event Requirements
The MVP should log meaningful events even before the mobile app feed is connected.
MVP events:
* WorldRuntimeStarted
* WorldRuntimeEnded
* UserEnteredWorld
* UserLeftRoom
* CapabilityAssigned
* AvatarLoaded
* SeatOccupied
* SeatLeft
* RecordingStarted

* RecordingStopped
* VerbatimMomentMarked
* PropInteracted
* ConnectionLost
* UserReconnected
Every meaningful world action should eventually become a structured WorldEvent.
Acceptance criteria:
* event schema exists
* events include WorldId, LocationId, WorldRuntimeId, actor user, timestamp, and metadata
* at least WorldRuntimeStarted, UserEnteredWorld, AvatarLoaded, and VerbatimMomentMarked can
be logged
* event system can later feed analytics, mobile app feed, world recaps, and moderation tools
## 17.10 Mobile App Future Bridge Requirements
The MVP should prepare for future mobile app integration.
The mobile app should eventually show content from inside WMCYN Online.
Rule:
**No normal real-world posting. Feed items should originate from in-world moments.**
Future feed item examples:
* live now in the Crib
* Verbatim clip
* world recap
* avatar update
* event announcement
* drop preview
* watch party highlight
* creator room update
MVP bridge requirements:
* game/world events use WorldId
* game/world events use LocationId
* game/world events use WorldRuntimeId
* future FeedItem model is considered
* direct mobile integration is not required yet

Acceptance criteria:
* PRD and ERD include FeedItem as future-facing entity
* WorldEvent model can support future mobile feed generation
* no current MVP decision blocks the mobile app companion strategy

# 19. Technical Philosophy
## 19.1 Core Systems in C++, Rapid Iteration Where Appropriate
The project should be C++-first for long-term system health, but not Blueprint-hostile.
The goal is not to avoid Blueprints for ego.
The goal is to keep the long-term system clean while still moving fast enough to validate the
product.
C++ should own:
* identity
* networking
* presence/capability state
* world runtime state

* avatar assignment
* event logging
* replicated interactions
* recording tools
* backend/event bridge
* core subsystems
Blueprints may be used for:
* layout iteration
* animation hookup
* UI layout
* visual tuning
* temporary prototypes
* designer-facing configuration
* level art placement
Blueprints should not own:
* core networking authority
* account logic
* avatar ownership
* world runtime lifecycle
* backend event model
* long-term product logic
## 19.2 Data-Driven Where Possible
The system should use data/config for:
* avatar definitions
* room definitions
* presence mode definitions
* capability definitions
* seat definitions
* camera marks
* interactable prop definitions
* lore object metadata
This keeps the project flexible.

# 26. Key Risks
## Risk 1: Scope Explosion
The world vision is massive. The MVP must stay one room.
Mitigation:
* keep one location only
* log future ideas separately

* do not build stores, concerts, or watch parties yet
## Risk 2: Unreal/Standalone Headset Complexity
Standalone headset performance and packaging may slow the project.
Mitigation:
* test standalone headset packaging before heavy art work
* optimize early
* use placeholder assets first
* avoid over-polishing before crossplay works
## Risk 3: Networking Complexity
VR networking is harder than normal multiplayer because head/hands update frequently.
Mitigation:
* start with simple replication
* test standalone headset + PCVR early
* avoid building single-player-only systems
* keep interactions minimal at first
## Risk 4: Blueprint Sprawl
Blueprints could become messy if used for core systems.
Mitigation:
* core systems in C++
* rapid iteration where appropriate
* Blueprint allowed for configuration and visual work
* avoid putting long-term authority logic in Blueprint
## Risk 5: Mobile App Disconnect
The mobile app could become separate from the world if not planned early.
Mitigation:
* define world events now
* use WorldId, LocationId, WorldRuntimeId from day one
* treat mobile feed as future consumer of in-world events

## Risk 6: Crib Loses Its Vibe
Porting from VRChat to Unreal may make the Crib feel too sterile or different.
Mitigation:
* preserve layout and landmarks first
* focus on couch/banner wall
* keep L.A.-inspired vibe
* add lore objects
* avoid over-studio design
## Risk 7: Avatar Scope Creep
Avatar systems can become expensive and endless.
Mitigation:
* one avatar per user
* manually assigned avatars
* no user uploads
* no avatar editor
* no outfit customization in MVP
## Risk 8: Voice Workflow Breaks Content
If voice quality is bad, the content may be unusable.
Mitigation:
* choose voice workflow before recording
* prefer hybrid voice for MVP
* prioritize clean audio over perfect immersion
* test audio before each recording run

# 27. Non-Negotiable Product Principles
## One Room First
The MVP is the Crib. Not the city. Not the platform. Not the whole world.

## Place Before Platform
The Crib must feel like a place people care about before WMCYN tries to make it a platform.
## WMCYN Is the First Customer
The first product must make WMCYN better at creating culture, content, and lore.
## Identity Matters
Users should not be random pawns. They should be account-bound avatars.
## Avatar Scope Stays Controlled
One user, one manually assigned avatar for MVP.
## Content Is Distribution
The world grows by creating content people want to watch.
## The Feed Comes From the World
The future mobile app should show in-world moments, not random posts from outside reality.
## Core Systems Stay Clean
Long-term systems should be C++ owned and data-aware, while iteration can use whatever is
fastest and safe.
## Lore Is Product
The Crib's story, vibe, and repeated appearances matter as much as features.
## Do Not Build Into a Wall
Every MVP system should leave room for future rooms, events, stores, avatar items, and mobile
app integration.
